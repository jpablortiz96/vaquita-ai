# Ethereum México · General Hybrid Track

## Why We're Building for Latin America

The team behind VaquitaAI is Latin American. We grew up watching our mothers, grandmothers, and aunts run vaquitas. We know the trust dynamics, the cultural rituals, and the very specific failure modes that English-language fintech misses entirely.

Every line of code in this project was written with **Doña Carmen** in mind — a 58-year-old grandmother in Querétaro who's been running a 12-person cundina for 20 years, who uses WhatsApp daily but has never opened MetaMask.

## The Latin American Angle

### 1. Spanish-First, Not Translated
The bot speaks Mexican Spanish natively. Not Google-translated. Not "neutral Latin Spanish." Real expressions: *"hacer una vaquita"*, *"le tocó a María"*, *"se voló con la lana"*, *"echamos una tanda"*.

### 2. WhatsApp as Primary Interface
95% of Mexicans use WhatsApp daily. Asking them to download an app is asking them to leave their comfort zone. The bot lives where they already are.

### 3. Pesos, Not Dollars
The unit of account is the Mexican peso, via Bitso's MXNB. Users never see USD pricing. Vaquita amounts, contributions, and payouts are all in pesos — exactly as the traditional vaquita has always worked.

### 4. Voice for Accessibility
Voice notifications in calm Spanish (via ElevenLabs) reach members with low literacy, visual impairments, or older relatives who prefer audio. The text version is always included, but the voice is the magic.

### 5. Trust Model Adapted to LATAM
Traditional credit scoring doesn't work for the informal economy. The AI risk scorer asks 4 friendly questions (occupation, monthly income, time in community, name) — exactly what a neighbor would ask before lending money.
